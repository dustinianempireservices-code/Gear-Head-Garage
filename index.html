What’s wrong with your vehicle?

This section is designed to catch customers who do not know the repair they need yet—they just know the vehicle is acting up. That tends to convert better than only listing services.

Check engine light on

A warning light can point to anything from a small issue to a larger drivability problem. The key is testing before guessing.

Noises, smells, or vibration

Grinding, squealing, shaking, clunking, or burning smells usually mean the vehicle needs attention before the problem grows.

Hard start or no start

If your vehicle is slow to crank, struggles to fire up, or does not start at all, the problem may be electrical, fuel-related, or sensor-based.

Loss of power

If the vehicle hesitates, feels sluggish, or is not driving the way it should, a deeper diagnosis may be needed.

Brakes feel wrong

Soft pedal, squeaking, grinding, vibration, or longer stopping distance should be inspected as soon as possible.

Leaks under the vehicle

Oil, coolant, transmission fluid, and brake fluid leaks can lead to bigger failures quickly if left alone.
